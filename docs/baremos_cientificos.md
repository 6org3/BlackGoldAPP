# Baremos Científicos — Physical Testing Normative Values for Youth Basketball

> [!NOTE]
> This document compiles normative reference values (baremos) from peer-reviewed scientific literature, international federations (FIBA, NBA, NSCA, ACSM), and validated fitness assessment programs (FitnessGram, President's Council on Fitness). Values are organized by age category: **Sub12** (10–12), **Sub15** (13–15), **Sub18** (16–18), **Senior** (18+). All values are for **male athletes** unless otherwise noted. Where basketball-specific norms exist, they are prioritized; otherwise, general youth athlete norms are used.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Biological maturation** (proximity to Peak Height Velocity) dramatically affects youth performance. These norms should be interpreted alongside maturity status. The NSCA and FIBA emphasize individual longitudinal tracking over rigid population comparisons.

---

## 1. STRENGTH (Fuerza)

### 1.1 Squat (Sentadilla) — Relative to Bodyweight

**Protocol:** Back squat to parallel depth (thighs parallel to floor). 1RM estimated or tested. Result expressed as ratio of 1RM ÷ bodyweight. Sub12 athletes should use goblet squat or bodyweight squat assessments for technical competency rather than 1RM testing.

**Sources:** NSCA Position Statement on Youth Resistance Training; NIH studies on elite youth soccer/sport athletes; Simplifaster.com athletic strength benchmarks.

> [!WARNING]
> The NSCA explicitly states there are **no official minimum strength standards** for adolescents. These values are derived from research on trained/elite youth athletes and should be treated as reference benchmarks, not pass/fail criteria. For Sub12, prioritize movement quality assessment over load testing.

| Tier | Sub12 (10–12) | Sub15 (13–15) | Sub18 (16–18) | Senior (18+) |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Excellent** | ≥ 0.8× BW | ≥ 1.6× BW | ≥ 2.0× BW | ≥ 2.0× BW |
| **Above Average** | 0.6–0.79× BW | 1.3–1.59× BW | 1.7–1.99× BW | 1.7–1.99× BW |
| **Average** | 0.4–0.59× BW | 1.0–1.29× BW | 1.4–1.69× BW | 1.4–1.69× BW |
| **Below Average** | 0.25–0.39× BW | 0.7–0.99× BW | 1.0–1.39× BW | 1.0–1.39× BW |
| **Poor** | < 0.25× BW | < 0.7× BW | < 1.0× BW | < 1.0× BW |

*Note for Sub12: Assess via goblet squat or bodyweight squat depth/form score rather than barbell 1RM.*

## 3. FLEXIBILITY / MOBILITY

### 3.1 Sit & Reach Test

**Protocol:** Standard sit & reach box. Zero point at feet (0 cm = touching toes). Best of 3 attempts.

**Sources:** FitnessGram; BrianMac Sports Coach; NIH pediatric flexibility studies.

| Tier | Sub12 (10–12) | Sub15 (13–15) | Sub18 (16–18) | Senior (18+) |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Excellent** | ≥ +8 cm | ≥ +10 cm | ≥ +14 cm | ≥ +17 cm |
| **Above Average** | +4 to +7 cm | +5 to +9 cm | +11 to +13 cm | +14 to +16 cm |
| **Average** | 0 to +3 cm | 0 to +4 cm | +7 to +10 cm | +7 to +13 cm |
| **Below Average** | −5 to −1 cm | −5 to −1 cm | +4 to +6 cm | +4 to +6 cm |
| **Poor** | < −5 cm | < −5 cm | < +4 cm | < +4 cm |

*Note: Boys often show temporary flexibility decreases during growth spurts (ages 11–14).*

---

### 3.2 Ankle Dorsiflexion (Weight-Bearing Lunge Test — WBLT)

**Protocol:** Knee tracks over second toe, lunges forward until knee touches wall without heel lifting. Measure distance from big toe to wall (cm). Side-to-side asymmetry > 1.5 cm is clinically significant.

**Sources:** NIH study on 693 non-injured youth basketball players (ages 12–17).

| Tier | All Categories (cm) | Classification |
|:---|:---:|:---|
| **Excellent** | ≥ 13.1 cm | Hypermobility (monitor for instability) |
| **Above Average** | 11.0–13.0 cm | Upper normal range |
| **Average (Normal)** | 8.4–10.9 cm | Adequate functional dorsiflexion |
| **Below Average** | 6.1–8.3 cm | Hypomobility — injury risk elevated |
| **Poor** | < 6.1 cm | Excessive hypomobility — corrective intervention needed |

**Clinical threshold:** ≥ 10 cm = Good | < 8–9 cm = Restricted (elevated ACL injury risk)

---

### 3.3 Hip Rotation ROM

**Protocol:** Measured with goniometer. Hip and knee flexed to 90°. Record both sides.

**Sources:** World Rugby musculoskeletal screening; NIH hip ROM in athletes.

| Measurement | Normal Range | Concern Threshold |
|:---|:---:|:---|
| **Hip Internal Rotation** | 30°–40° | < 25° = restricted |
| **Hip External Rotation** | 40°–60° | < 35° = restricted |
| **Total Arc (IR + ER)** | 80°–95° | < 70° = clinically significant |
| **Side-to-side asymmetry** | < 8° difference | > 10° = injury risk flag |

---

### 3.4 Shoulder Rotation ROM

**Protocol:** Measured at 90° shoulder abduction. Patient supine.

**Sources:** NIH shoulder ROM in overhead athletes; Eric Cressey clinical recommendations.

| Measurement | Normal Range | Basketball Adaptation |
|:---|:---:|:---|
| **Shoulder External Rotation** | 80°–100° | Dominant arm may show 5–10° more ER |
| **Shoulder Internal Rotation** | 60°–80° | Dominant arm may show 5–10° less IR |
| **Total Rotational Arc** | 160°–180° | Should be symmetrical (±10°) |
| **GIRD (IR deficit)** | < 15° difference | > 20° = pathological |

---

### 3.5 Rebuilding Milo Protocol (Methodology)

**Reference:** Dr. Aaron Horschig, *Rebuilding Milo* (Squat University).

A comprehensive self-assessment and rehabilitation system: Ankle → Knee → Hip → Spine → Shoulder → Elbow. This is a **methodology/framework** rather than a scored test — it produces categorical findings (adequate/limited/restricted) per joint region. Use Milo-style movement screens as part of pre-season musculoskeletal screening alongside the ROM tests above.

## 6. Endurance / Resistencia (Sub-Pilar Físico)

> [!NOTE]
> The tables in this section follow the club engine's threshold convention: **four ascending cut-points `[t1, t2, t3, t4]`** per cell, marking the boundaries between the five tiers. For *more-is-better* tests: < t1 = Poor | t1–t2 = Below Average | t2–t3 = Average | t3–t4 = Above Average | ≥ t4 = Excellent. For *less-is-better* (timed) tests the reading is inverted: ≤ t1 = Excellent … > t4 = Poor. Development-level layer: **Desarrollo** = population norm; **Micro** −10% / **Elite** +10% demand (internal club rule aligned to Vinueza's percentile framework; recalibrate with the club's own data).

### 6.1 Course Navette / 20m Shuttle Run (Léger)

**Protocol:** Shuttle run between two lines 20 m apart, paced by audio (speed increases each palier/stage). The test ends when the athlete fails to reach the line on the beep twice in a row. Record the last completed palier (half-paliers allowed, e.g. 5.5). Estimates aerobic capacity (VO₂max) — the base of the intermittent endurance basketball demands.

**Sources:** Léger et al. 1988 (J Sports Sci) — original 20 m shuttle run protocol, the most validated aerobic field test in pediatric populations; Tomkinson et al. 2017 (Br J Sports Med, n≈1.14M) — international norms ages 9–17; Vinueza (Cap. II) — sensitive phase for aerobic endurance from ages 9–10. Documented sexual dimorphism → gender layer.

### 6.2 Yo-Yo Intermittent Recovery Test — Level 1

**Protocol:** Intermittent 2×20 m shuttle runs with 10 s of active recovery between bouts, paced by audio with progressively increasing speed. Record the last completed level. Evaluates the ability to repeatedly perform intense intermittent exercise — the physiological profile of basketball play.

**Sources:** Bangsbo, Iaia & Krustrup 2008 (Sports Med) — Yo-Yo IR test validity and physiological response in intermittent sports.

Cut-points by gender (age wildcard **"Todas"** — applies to all buckets). Unit: level reached (more is better):

|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Masculino** | 12 | 14 | 16 | 18 |
| **Femenino** | 10 | 12 | 14 | 16 |

### 6.3 Carrera 600 m (Sub12) & 1000 m (Sub15) — Batería Vinueza

**Protocol:** On a track or court with measured distance, run 600 m (ages 9–10, bucket **Sub12 only**) or 1000 m (ages 11–12, bucket **Sub15 only**) in the shortest possible time. Standing start, manual stopwatch. Record total time in **seconds** (less is better). The 600 m serves as field alternative to the Course Navette when no audio is available.

**Sources:** Batería ecuatoriana de detección de talento (E. Vinueza Tapia / **Romero Frómeta 2013, FEDENADOR**, Tablas No.4–7, N=1266). Manual original contrastado celda por celda (2026-07-22).
